Carl vs Wilson

Two Teenagers, Two AI Philosophies

A deep analysis of how your AI stack choice can define the future of your company

For Visionary CTOs

Originally published on pragmatismo.com.br

Carl, age 17

Loves Big Tech Marketing

Carl's Profile:

Carl represents the classic corporate mindset: "If it works for everyone, it works for me." He genuinely believes that following the path already paved by millions is the smartest strategy. After all, why reinvent the wheel when Microsoft (superhero) has already done the work?

  • Prompts copied from ChatGPT — "Why create when I can copy?"
  • Official documentation is law — Only trusts it if it has the Microsoft logo
  • 5-minute setup — Proud of not thinking too much
  • Stack Overflow as gospel — The community always has the answer
  • Automatic updates — Lets Big Tech decide for him
Carl doesn't realize he's building exactly the same product as his competitors...
Reliable ✓ Quick Setup ⚡ Commodity 📦

Wilson, age 17

The Independent Innovator

Wilson's Profile:

Wilson is someone who can't use something without first understanding how it works inside. He sees every problem as an opportunity to create something truly new, even if it means taking more time.

  • Diverse and local LLMs — "My data, my rules"
  • Prompts nobody thought of — Experiments with disruptive approaches
  • Completely original code — Stack oriented to spending less of the planet
  • Personalized infrastructure — Every decision is intentional
  • Tailor-made algorithms — Real competitive advantage
Wilson is building what will become the industry standard in 3-5 years...
Creative Customizable Innovative

Two Philosophies, Two Results

The difference between Carl and Wilson is not just in the tools they choose, but in the mindset they bring to problem-solving. Each approach has its advantages and represents a completely different business strategy.

Carl represents companies that value stability, predictability, and rapid implementation. His approach works well for organizations that need consistent results and have well-established processes.

Wilson represents companies that seek competitive differentiation through technological innovation. His approach is ideal for organizations that want to create unique solutions and have full control over their technology stack.

Big Tech World: The Comfort Zone

"Why think when BigTechs have already thought for you?"

Herd Security

ISO, SOC2, GDPR certifications... all very impressive on paper. But when everyone uses the same solution, hackers also know exactly where to attack. It's like using the same lock on every house in town.

Predictability... or Trap?

Started cheap, right? But when your company grows and becomes dependent, prices magically increase. And then you discover that migrating costs more than paying the premium price.

Community... of Clones

Extensive documentation? Great! Millions of developers doing the same thing? Your company becomes just another in the crowd. Zero differentiation, zero competitive advantage.

Reality: You're not buying technology, you're renting dependency.

Open Source Approach

Differentiated Innovation

The ability to create truly unique solutions that don't exist in the market. Enables developing specific features that become sustainable competitive advantages, difficult to replicate.

Total Autonomy

Complete control over data, algorithms, and infrastructure. No dependency on third parties for critical functionality, with the ability to modify any aspect of the system as needed.

Personalized Scalability

Architecture optimized for the company's specific needs, resulting in better performance and optimized costs. The ability to scale only the necessary components.
