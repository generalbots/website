Digital Twins: Your AI-Powered Digital Replica

Author: Pragmatismo Team

What Is a Digital Twin?

A digital twin is a virtual replica of a physical process, system, or entity that mirrors its real-world counterpart in real time. Originally developed for aerospace and manufacturing — where NASA used twin models to simulate spacecraft behavior under conditions impossible to reproduce on Earth — the concept has expanded far beyond industrial applications. With the convergence of IoT sensors, real-time data pipelines, and large language models, digital twins are now accessible to businesses and individuals who would never have considered them a decade ago.

LLM Orchestrators are advanced AI systems that can automate practically any digital task, functioning as an intelligent and incredibly versatile personal assistant. Imagine an AI assistant that:

  • •Thinks and acts exactly like you
  • •Works 24h without rest
  • •Responds to messages on your behalf
  • •Automates repetitive tasks
  • •Collects and processes information from multiple sources

BASIC Code Configuration

REM Configuracao inicial do seu Digital Twin
REM Voce pode programar ou usar linguagem natural

TALK "Ola! Sou sua assistente digital. Vamos me configurar?"

REM Preferencias pessoais
DISLIKE "abacaxi" REASON "Nao gosto do sabor"
DISLIKE "acordar cedo" REASON "Prefiro dormir ate mais tarde"

REM Disponibilidade
AVAILABILITY "sabado" TIME "20:00-23:00" ACTION "pode sair"
AVAILABILITY "domingo" TIME "14:00-18:00" ACTION "pode receber visitas"

REM Respostas automaticas
AUTO_REPLY "vai sair sabado?" ANSWER "Estou livre sim!" WHEN "sabado 20:00-23:00"
AUTO_REPLY "gosta de abacaxi?" ANSWER "Nao gosto de abacaxi de maneira alguma!"

Your Clone Digital

Your Digital Twin learns from you and can respond to messages automatically on WhatsApp, email, and other platforms, following your personal preferences.

Example: If you do not like pineapple, your Digital Twin will automatically respond: "I do not like pineapple at all!" when asked about it.

What you can teach:

  • •Food preferences and hobbies
  • •Hours when you are available
  • •Answers to frequently asked questions
  • •How to handle invitations and requests

What it avoids:

  • •Taking on commitments without confirmation
  • •Responding about sensitive subjects
  • •Making important decisions alone

Advanced Customization with BASIC

Conditional Responses

REM If asked about going out on Saturday
IF WEEKDAY = "sabado" AND TIME >= "20:00" THEN
  TALK "Estou livre para sair!"
ELSE
  TALK "Nao estou disponivel nesse horario"
END IF

Your Digital Twin can make decisions based on multiple factors

Learning Preferences

REM Learn from interactions
ON MESSAGE "gosta de abacaxi?"
  IF USER_RESPONSE = "nao" THEN
    DISLIKE "abacaxi"
  END IF

It can learn automatically from your responses

First Steps with BASIC

Essential Commands:

  • TALK "message" — Displays a message to the user
  • HEAR variable — Captures the user's response
  • IF condition THEN ... ELSE ... END IF — Makes decisions
  • name = "value" — Stores information in variables

Complete Example:

REM Welcome program in BASIC
REM This is a comment - the computer ignores it

TALK "Bem-vindo ao mundo do BASIC!"
TALK "Qual e o seu nome?"
HEAR nome

TALK "Quantos anos voce tem?"
HEAR idade

IF idade < 18 THEN
  TALK "Que legal comecar cedo, " + nome + "!"
ELSE
  TALK "Nunca e tarde para aprender, " + nome + "!"
END IF

In Business

In business, digital twins transform how organizations monitor and optimize operations. A manufacturing plant can run a twin that ingests sensor data from every machine, detects anomalies before they become failures, and recommends maintenance schedules that minimize downtime. A logistics company can simulate route changes against real-time traffic and weather data, comparing predicted outcomes before committing resources. The predictive capability is what separates a digital twin from a dashboard: a twin does not just show you what is happening — it models what will happen next, and it can act on those predictions autonomously when configured to do so.

The practical barrier to digital twins has always been integration complexity. Connecting sensors, normalizing data formats, building inference pipelines, and exposing outputs through usable interfaces required dedicated engineering teams. General Bots collapses this stack. Its BASIC programming model lets domain experts — not just developers — define twin behaviors. Its multi-channel deployment means a twin can surface insights on WhatsApp for a floor manager, in Microsoft Teams for an executive, and via email for a partner — all from the same bot logic. The result is a digital twin that does not live in a silo but operates wherever decisions are made.
