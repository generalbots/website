What is an LLM? Deconstructing the Model.

A didactic technical guide to Large Language Models (LLMs). Understanding parameters, tokens, and the shift toward sovereign AI search orchestration with GLM 5.1.

A Large Language Model (LLM) is currently defined as a "Statistical Prediction Node." It is an architecture of neural weights trained on the corpus of human knowledge to predict the next token in a sequence. To reach an investor-ready understanding, we must look past the "Chat" interface and analyze the didactic reality of the weights and biases that power the GLM 5.1 era.

Tokens and Parameters: The Units of Intelligence

Intelligence in an LLM is measured by **Parameters** (the connections) and **Tokens** (the fragments of text). A model with 70 billion parameters has a higher resolution of understanding than a 7 billion parameter model. However, didactic efficiency is not just about size; it's about how those parameters are orchestrated. The "Architecture of Intelligence" focuses on maximizing the signal-to-noise ratio in every prompt.

AI Search: The De Facto standard for LLM Utility

An LLM without data is just a creative writer. **AI Search** (or RAG - Retrieval-Augmented Generation) is the de facto standard for making LLMs useful in a corporate environment. By indexing your sovereign data, the model stops "hallucinating" and starts providing high-fidelity answers based on *your* records. This is the difference between a toy and a tool.
