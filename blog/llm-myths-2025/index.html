LLM Myths 2025

A didactic deconstruction of the misconceptions holding enterprise AI back.

March 28, 2025

Originally published on pragmatismo.com.br

The enterprise AI space is saturated with high-fidelity marketing that obscures underlying technical realities. To build a sovereign infrastructure, one must first dismantle the myths of the proprietary stack. Here are the three most dangerous misconceptions in 2025.

Myth 1

"Scale is the Only Moat"

The dominant narrative claims that only companies with the largest clusters and the most parameters will survive the AI revolution. This is a convenient story for hyperscalers selling compute.

Reality

Orchestration Depth > Parameter Count

While parameter count matters for general knowledge, Orchestration Depth is the true competitive advantage for enterprises. A smaller, sovereign model (DeepSeek, Llama 3) running within a targeted orchestration framework consistently outperforms a massive proprietary model that lacks context-specific deterministic logic. Precision beats scale when the task is specific and the data is proprietary.

Myth 2

"SaaS is More Secure"

This is perhaps the most dangerous misconception in enterprise AI procurement. The argument goes: cloud providers have dedicated security teams, so your data is safer with them than on your own infrastructure.

Reality

True Security is Sovereignty

In a SaaS model, your data — the Architecture of Intelligence for your business — is processed on third-party hardware. True security is found only in sovereignty. On-premises deployment ensures your data and models never leave your firewall. No third-party access. No data exfiltration risk. No compliance uncertainty.

Myth 3

"AI is a Commodity"

The belief that all AI models are interchangeable and the only differentiator is price. If this were true, every organization using AI would achieve the same results.

Reality

Connectors Create Value Delta

The delta in performance comes from the Connectors. How you link your models to SQL databases, Word documents, legacy APIs, and proprietary data sources — via BASIC scripts — is where the value is created. General Bots is not a tool; it is a system for creating non-commoditized intelligence assets that are deeply integrated with your existing infrastructure.

The Path to Sovereignty

Debunking these myths is the first step toward a strategic exit from Big Tech dependency. Organizations that internalize these realities can build AI infrastructure that is:

Owner-controlled — Your models, your data, your infrastructure, your rules
Deterministically verifiable — Every output can be traced to a verified source
Deeply integrated — Connected to your existing enterprise systems, not abstracted away
Cost-predictable — No per-token surprise bills or API pricing changes
"Own your stack. Own your intelligence. The myths of the proprietary era are designed to keep you dependent — sovereignty is the only exit."
